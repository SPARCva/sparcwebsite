February 28, 2026 Recognition

SPARC Recognized on Serve Virginia Honor Roll for Outstanding Volunteerism

Specially Adapted Resource Centers (SPARC) has been named to the Winter 2026 Serve Virginia Honor Roll in recognition of the organization's outstanding contributions to volunteerism and community service. SPARC was nominated by Board Chair Ellen Dyke, whose tireless leadership continues to drive the organization's mission forward.

The Serve Virginia Honor Roll celebrates individuals and organizations across the Commonwealth whose dedication to service strengthens communities and enhances quality of life. SPARC's inclusion on the Honor Roll reflects years of committed work providing dignity, belonging, and meaningful engagement for adults with severe and multiple disabilities throughout Northern Virginia.

"We are deeply honored by this recognition from Serve Virginia," said Ellen Dyke, SPARC Board Chair. "This award belongs to every volunteer, staff member, family, and community partner who makes SPARC's work possible. Together, we are building a more inclusive Virginia where every adult is valued and connected."

SPARC operates six community-integrated day program centers across Northern Virginia, serving adults with severe and multiple disabilities through innovative leisure-learning formats that build life skills, foster independence, and reduce social isolation. From adaptive art and music therapy to community outings and workforce development, SPARC creates environments where participants thrive.
