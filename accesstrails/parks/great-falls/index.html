Great Falls National Park

Accessibility rating: Mostly Accessible

Near SPARC McLean

Unique Feature: Accessible Viewing Area of the Falls

This park has a specifically designated accessible viewing area of the falls at Overlook 3.

Accessibility Ratings

Each amenity was scored against an ADA-based checklist. N/A means the amenity is absent; parks are not penalized for it. See the methodology for how scores were assigned.

Parking

6/6

There is plenty of accessible parking with adequate maneuvering space near the trail entrance that leads to the visitor's center.

Trails

6/7
  • The trail is small gravel and mostly flat, but has some bumps that have to be maneuvered around.
  • The path leading to the overlook area has an accessible ramp.
  • Note that only Overlook 3 has an accessible viewing area.
  • The falls are partially visible at wheelchair height at Overlook 2.

Bathrooms

7/7

There is a fully accessible bathroom down the ramp from the visitor's center and near the snack bar.

Visitors Center/Clubhouse

5/5
  • The visitor's center is very nice with guides and maneuverable space.
  • Note that the electronic door was broken as of mid-August 2025, but there are guides staffing the center during park hours who could assist with the doors.

Picnic Shelter & Seating Areas

4/5
  • There are accessible tables, however they are near the bathrooms which, during the hotter days in summer, did not smell pleasant.
  • All of the other picnic tables are on the grass and may be difficult to traverse in a wheelchair.

Signage

3/6

There is plenty of clear, directional and designated signage throughout the park.
