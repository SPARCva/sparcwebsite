About the Summit

A Call to Conscience: Advancing Dignity for Adults with Severe and Multiple Disabilities

Adults with severe and multiple disabilities experience some of the highest levels of social isolation, limited access to meaningful community engagement, and families living with constant financial, emotional, and caregiving strain.

The SPARC/GW Summit keynote will be delivered by Virginia Senator Tim Kaine and brings together individuals with lived experience, families, advocates, researchers, policymakers, and community partners for a one-day working forum focused on solutions and collective action.

Sponsorship reflects a shared commitment to designing a future that expands dignity and possibility for some of our most isolated individuals.

Call for Posters

Share your research, insights, and innovations at the 2026 SPARC Summit

Join SPARC and George Washington University for a landmark gathering of thought leaders, policymakers, healthcare professionals, families, and self-advocates committed to one simple truth: Dignity is not a privilege – it's a right.

Submission Guidelines

Please submit a 200–300 word abstract. Possible topics include:

  • ✓ Examining limiting narratives
  • ✓ Recognizing systemic barriers
  • ✓ Universal design & technology
  • ✓ Health equity
  • ✓ Any relevant topic

The authors of the top selected abstracts will have an opportunity to present their posters in person. There will also be a virtual poster session for other selected abstracts.

Deadline: April 27th, 2026
